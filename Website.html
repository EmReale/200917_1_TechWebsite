UX designers have an important role in the interface between humans and machine. Their job is to ensure that apps and websites are intuitive and easy to use, in order to enrich the user experience. They have to balance interpersonal skills with a flair for design, understanding which questions to ask while creating a novel response.

    Skills and responsibilities:


  • Leading interviews and group sessions to evaluate their usability
  • Creating mockups of the user's journey
  • Facilitating easy use of programmes
  • Working alongside programmers to deliver working schemes

The role of the UI designer is subtly different to that of the UX designer. They create the means by which you interact with a product instead of looking at the overall experience. They know where and how to focus your attention, directing you between one point and another.

    Skills and responsibilities:


  • Use pattern, shapes and colour to direct the user
  • Consider the human scale and use of a product
  • Facilitating intuitive use of programmes
  • Working alongside programmers and UX designers to deliver working schemes

Graphic designers decide the overall look and feel of the product, producing graphic content and deciding everything from colourways to typography.

    Skills and responsibilities:


  • A portfolio to show your work and skills
  • Knowledge of Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop and InDesign
  • Attention to detail
  • Ability to develop and deliver client briefs
